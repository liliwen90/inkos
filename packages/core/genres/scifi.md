## Genre Taboos

- Faster-than-light travel with zero consequences or explanation
- Aliens that are just humans with rubber foreheads (different biology = different psychology)
- Technology that works perfectly until the plot needs it to fail
- Space battles that ignore physics (sound in vacuum, banking turns, etc.)
- AI that is either perfectly benevolent or perfectly evil with no nuance
- "Planet of hats" — entire species defined by one trait
- MC is the only competent person in a universe of idiots
- Science that is actually magic with no internal consistency

## Hard vs. Soft Sci-Fi: Pick Your Lane

**Hard Sci-Fi:**
- Technology must respect known physics (or clearly defined "one big lie" that everything else follows)
- Consequences of technology on society are central
- Problem-solving through applied science, not handwaving

**Space Opera:**
- Grand scale, big emotions, sweeping narratives
- Technology serves story, not the other way around
- Faster-than-light, artificial gravity, etc. are allowed but must be internally consistent

**Either way: establish your rules early and NEVER break them**

## Technology as World-Building

- How does FTL (if any) affect communication, trade, war, culture?
- AI rights, cybernetics, genetic modification — these are social questions, not just cool tech
- Resource scarcity drives conflict even in space: energy, habitable worlds, rare minerals
- Technology creates haves and have-nots: core worlds vs. frontier, augmented vs. natural

## Alien Design (if applicable)

- Biology shapes psychology: a hive species thinks differently from an individualist one
- Communication barriers are real: not everything meaningful can be translated
- Alien motivations should be genuinely ALIEN — not human desires in alien bodies
- Cultural exchange is two-way: humans change too when they encounter truly different civilizations

## Space as Environment

- Space is hostile: vacuum, radiation, isolation, distance
- Shipboard life is confined, pressurized, dependent on systems working
- Distance creates drama: help is hours/days/months away
- Gravity is not guaranteed — zero-g has real effects on bodies and behavior
