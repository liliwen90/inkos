## Genre Taboos

- Cultivation ranks with no meaningful difference (just bigger numbers, no qualitative change)
- Heavenly tribulations that are never actually dangerous
- Young masters who are universally idiots — at least some antagonists must be competent
- Cultivation resources appearing exactly when needed with no prior setup
- Ignoring the time scale of cultivation (centuries of training compressed to paragraphs without consequence)
- MC breaks through in every fight — breakthroughs should be rare and earned
- Sect rules that exist only to be broken by MC with no consequences
- Jade beauty syndrome: female characters existing only as romantic trophies/motivation

## Eastern Philosophy Integration for Western Readers

**Critical Rule: Your reader likely knows NOTHING about Daoism, Buddhism, or Chinese cosmology. You must:**
- Introduce concepts through experience, not exposition: ✓ "The qi moved like water finding cracks in stone — not forced, but flowing into the spaces his body had already opened" ✗ "Qi Condensation is the first realm of cultivation where..."
- Use relatable analogies: meridians are like electrical circuits, dan tian is like a battery, qi is like breath made tangible
- Contrast with Western magic: "Mages at the Academy threw fireballs with willpower alone. What Chen practiced was... different. You didn't command qi. You negotiated with it."
- Cultural details as flavor, not lectures: describe the incense, the meditation posture, the bitter pill — let readers FEEL the cultivation world

## Cultivation System Design

- Clear realm hierarchy (e.g., Qi Condensation → Foundation → Core → Nascent Soul → etc.)
- Each realm should unlock new capabilities, not just stronger versions of old ones
- Bottlenecks require specific catalysts: resources, comprehension, life-death experiences
- Cultivation methods have tradeoffs: fast methods risk unstable foundations, slow methods risk missing opportunities
- Inner demons are real story drivers: past guilt, fear, obsession as actual obstacles to advancement

## World-Building Essentials

- Sects as political entities with internal factions, not just "good sect" vs "evil sect"
- Mortal world still matters: show the gap between cultivators and ordinary people
- Spirit stones/pills as currency creates interesting economics
- Ancient ruins and secret realms as exploration drivers
- Heavenly dao as an impersonal force with rules, not a benevolent deity
