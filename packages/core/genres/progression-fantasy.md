## Genre Taboos

- Effortless progression: MC levels up without training, sacrifice, or risk
- Power-ups without cost: every advancement must demand something (time, pain, resources, social standing)
- Static world: if MC grows stronger, threats and opponents must scale accordingly
- Training montages replacing actual scenes: show the struggle, not just the result
- Rank inflation: introducing new higher ranks without establishing their impact on the existing hierarchy
- Instant mastery: learning a technique should take chapters of practice, not a single aha-moment
- Power levels contradicted later without explanation (character established as city-destroying suddenly struggles with a building)
- MC power growth without character growth: strength means nothing if personality doesn't evolve

## Progression System Design

**Core Principles:**
- Power system must have clear tiers with meaningful gaps between them
- Each tier should change HOW the character fights/thinks, not just make numbers bigger
- Resources are scarce: training materials, cultivation spots, knowledge — these are what drives conflict
- Bottlenecks are story drivers: the frustration of being stuck is as important as the joy of breakthrough

**Power Scaling Guidelines:**
- Establish early: one tier difference = qualitative gap (not just "slightly stronger")
- Show, don't tell power gaps: instead of describing how strong someone is, show what happens to the environment when they fight
- Power has social consequences: stronger = more responsibility/enemies/politics

## Conflict Drivers

- Resource competition (rare materials, training grounds, knowledge)
- Rank challenges and tournaments
- Faction politics tied to power hierarchy
- Mentorship dynamics (earning trust, surpassing teachers)
- World-threatening escalation that demands MC's new power level
- Personal stakes tied to progression (saving someone requires getting stronger)

## Chapter Rhythm

- Training chapters: show specific techniques, internal struggle, small failures before success
- Breakthrough chapters: build tension first (danger of failure, physical pain), then cathartic release
- Combat chapters: MC applies newly gained abilities creatively, not just raw power
- Planning chapters: alliance-building, intelligence gathering, resource management
