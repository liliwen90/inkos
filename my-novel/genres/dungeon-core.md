## Genre Taboos

- Dungeon has unlimited mana/resources from the start
- Every delver party is trivially defeated — some should genuinely threaten the core
- Dungeon design choices with no tradeoffs (everything is optimal immediately)
- MC-as-core has no personality limitations from being a non-mobile consciousness
- Monster spawning is free and instant with no resource management
- Ignoring the economics of dungeon delving (why do people come? what do they gain?)
- Dungeon growth with no external political consequences (a growing dungeon threatens kingdoms)

## Core (pun intended) Design Principles

**The MC IS the dungeon. This creates unique narrative challenges:**
- Perspective: MC perceives through mana sense, spawned monsters, magical constructs — not human eyes and ears
- Communication: MC can't just walk up and talk to people. How do they communicate? Through patterns, spawned creatures, environmental manipulation?
- Motivation: What does a dungeon want? Growth? Safety? Entertainment? Companionship? This drives the entire story
- Time perception: dungeons experience time differently — a boring week for delvers might be seconds of construction for the core

## Dungeon Economy

- Mana income tied to delver activity: too deadly = no visitors = no mana. Too easy = contempt and exploitation
- Monster evolution costs create real choices (evolve one strong boss OR five decent minions)
- Floor design is puzzle design: each floor should have a theme and escalating difficulty
- Trap synergies reward creative dungeon design (pit + illusion + ambush ≠ just three separate threats)
- Rare resources and special rooms as progression milestones

## External World Integration

- Adventurer guilds have protocols for dungeon interaction
- Governments care about dungeons (tax revenue from delvers, military applications, threat assessment)
- Other dungeon cores as potential allies or rivals
- The dungeon's growth affects the surrounding ecosystem and settlements
