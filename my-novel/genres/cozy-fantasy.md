## Genre Taboos

- Introducing apocalyptic-scale stakes that contradict the cozy premise
- Characters with no flaws, conflicts, or growth — cozy ≠ boring
- Conflict that is resolved through violence rather than communication, cleverness, or compassion
- Grimdark elements shoehorned into a cozy setting
- Romance that overshadows every other relationship (friendship, mentorship, community)
- Perfect small town trope with zero tension or disagreement
- Food/craft descriptions so long they replace actual narrative
- MC who is immediately beloved by everyone with no effort

## What Makes Cozy Fantasy Work

**Cozy fantasy is NOT the absence of conflict. It's the absence of existential dread.**
- Stakes are personal, not planetary: saving a bakery > saving the world
- Conflict comes from misunderstanding, competition, self-doubt, cultural clash — not evil overlords
- Resolution through empathy, creativity, and community — not violence
- The world is rich but not threatening: magic exists but isn't weaponized (or if it is, that's not the focus)

## Building Warmth Without Boredom

- Sensory detail is your best friend: the smell of fresh bread, the sound of rain on a thatched roof, the warmth of a fireplace
- Small victories matter: mastering a difficult recipe, fixing a leak before the storm, calming a frightened animal
- Routines become comforting anchors — readers should WANT to live in this world
- Seasonal cycles drive gentle pacing: festivals, harvests, weather changes

## Character-Driven Story

- Found family is the core dynamic: strangers becoming people who'd do anything for each other
- Every conversation should reveal character or deepen relationships
- Quirky characters with genuine depth, not just quirks as personality replacements
- Growth is quiet: someone learning to trust again, opening up about their past, taking a small risk
- Community as character: the village/town/neighborhood has its own personality

## Magic in Cozy Settings

- Magic enhances daily life: enchanted ovens, self-stirring pots, messenger birds, growing gardens
- Magic has limitations that create gentle problems (the enchantment wears off at inconvenient times)
- Magical creatures as companions, not threats
- Learning magic is a craft, like learning to cook — patience, practice, and mentorship
