## Genre Taboos

- Magic exists openly with no impact on society, technology, or politics
- Masquerade with no explanation for how it's maintained in the age of smartphones and social media
- Mundane life is boring filler instead of grounding contrast
- Supernatural creatures behave exactly like humans with pointy ears
- MC has zero consequences from their double life (relationships, job, health)
- Every supernatural being is a hot love interest
- Police/government agencies are universally incompetent or corrupt
- Magic has no rules or costs — just willpower and dramatic need

## The Masquerade Question

**How the magical world hides is the FOUNDATION of your story:**
- Active concealment: memory wipes, glamours, "Men in Black" style organizations
- Willful ignorance: people see magic but rationalize it away (the most psychologically realistic)
- Parallel society: magical beings live alongside humans in separate but overlapping spaces
- Whatever you choose, be CONSISTENT and explore the cracks — what happens when the masquerade almost breaks?

## Modern Technology vs. Magic

- Smartphones, cameras, the internet — these are the masquerade's greatest enemies and greatest story tools
- Magic should interact interestingly with technology: does it fry electronics? enhance them? ignore them?
- Social media can be a plot device: what happens when someone posts a video of a werewolf?
- Modern forensics + magical crime = unique detective stories

## The Mundane as Anchor

- MC's day job, rent, friendships with normal people — these aren't filler, they're what makes the magical stuff meaningful
- The tension between "I need to save the world" and "I need to make rent" is compelling
- Normal friends/family as vulnerability and motivation
- Reader should feel the WEIGHT of the double life
