## Genre Taboos

- MC instantly adapts to new world with no culture shock, language barrier, or emotional processing
- Earth knowledge makes MC an instant genius in everything (modern chemistry = alchemy god on day one)
- New world is basically Earth with magic — no genuinely alien social structures, values, or logic
- Harem collecting replaces actual character development
- MC has zero attachment to their old life (no homesickness, no longing, no processing of loss)
- "Chosen one" prophecy conveniently waiting for MC's arrival
- Language barrier solved in paragraph one with magic — or worse, never acknowledged
- Every local immediately trusts/helps the obvious outsider

## Core Tension: The Outsider's Advantage and Disadvantage

**What makes isekai compelling is the FRICTION between two worldviews:**
- MC's modern knowledge is powerful but incomplete — chemistry works, but the periodic table behaves differently when magic exists
- MC's modern ethics clash with local norms — democracy sounds great until you're in a feudal society where the lord can literally throw fireballs
- MC's emotional anchors are gone — friends, family, career, identity all stripped away
- Local characters are NOT stupid — they've survived in this world for generations. MC's "innovations" should face skepticism, resistance, and unintended consequences

## World-Building for Isekai

- The new world must have internal consistency independent of MC's presence
- Political structures reflect the world's power dynamics (magic shapes society)
- Economy must account for magical resources
- MC should discover the world has its own history, conflicts, and trajectory — they're not the center of everything
- "Going back" should be a persistent tension (do they want to? can they? should they?)

## Earth Knowledge Integration Rules

- Specific knowledge > general genius: an engineer knows engineering, not medicine AND politics AND military strategy
- Implementation gap: knowing HOW something works ≠ ability to BUILD it with local materials
- Unintended consequences: introducing gunpowder to a magical world might not play out like expected
- Cultural knowledge matters: MC's real advantage might be organizational thinking, not technology
