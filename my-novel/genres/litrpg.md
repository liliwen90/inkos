## Genre Taboos

- The System is omnipotent: gives the MC whatever they need with zero cost or effort
- Loot/rewards wildly disproportionate to challenge (kill a rat, get a legendary sword)
- Status screens replace actual storytelling — walls of stat blocks instead of narrative
- Every problem solved by the System; MC has no agency, judgment, or tactical thinking
- System has no cost, no cooldowns, no limitations — pure cheat code
- Gacha/loot boxes always roll perfect; no failure, no junk items
- System rules contradicted later (item previously unsellable suddenly appears in shop)
- MC treats the System with pure gratitude — never questions, never pushes back
- Eastern cultivation terminology used without translation or worldbuilding context
- "Chosen One" status handed out with zero narrative justification

## Eastern Mysticism Integration — The Jade Protocol

This is what makes YOUR LitRPG different. You are not writing generic Western GameLit. You are writing LitRPG infused with Eastern philosophy, mythology, and cultivation concepts — presented through a System interface that Western readers can understand.

**The Jade Protocol Principles:**
- The System draws power from Eastern cosmology: Qi meridians, Five Elements (Wood/Fire/Earth/Metal/Water), Yin-Yang balance, Heavenly Tribulations, Karmic Debt
- Cultivation ranks map to System tiers, but are expressed as Game levels: ✗ "Qi Condensation Realm" → ✓ "Tier 2: Qi Condensation — Meridian channels online. Base mana regeneration +40%"
- Skills and abilities reference Eastern martial arts, Daoist techniques, Buddhist concepts — but through System tooltips that explain them to the reader
- ✓ "[Skill Acquired: Iron Shirt Qigong — Lv.1] Channels internal qi to harden skin. Damage reduction +15%. Warning: Prolonged use causes meridian strain."
- Monsters and bosses draw from Chinese/Japanese/Korean mythology: Jiangshi, Yaoguai, Oni, Gumiho, Naga — not just Western orcs and goblins
- Alchemy and crafting use traditional Chinese medicine concepts: herbal combinations, elemental balance, refinement through the Five Phases
- Formations (arrays/battle formations from Daoist magic) are translated as System-recognized tactical buffs

**Cultural Knowledge Gap = Your Advantage:**
- Western readers are FASCINATED by Eastern mysticism but have almost zero knowledge — every concept you introduce is fresh and exotic
- Explain just enough to intrigue, never enough to bore: ✓ "The meridian maps weren't like anything in his anatomy textbooks. Twelve primary channels, eight extraordinary vessels — and the System was telling him three of his were completely blocked."
- Contrast Eastern and Western approaches: "His party's healer used holy magic; crude, effective, like a sledgehammer. What Carl was learning was acupuncture — finding the exact pressure point where a thread of qi could do what a tidal wave of mana couldn't."
- Food and alchemy are cultural anchors: describe the smell of star anise in a qi-enhancing broth, the ritual of tea ceremony as a meditation buff, the bitter medicinal taste of a breakthrough pill

## System Rules

- System panels: clean, concise, consistent format. Use `[brackets]` or `<<angle brackets>>` — pick ONE and stick with it throughout
- Stat displays in prose: MAX 3 lines per appearance. More than that is padding. ✗ Full character sheet dump → ✓ "Strength ticked up again — he could tell because his grip accidentally crushed the wooden cup"
- Numerical progression must be internally consistent: stats, levels, skill points follow a defined growth curve with diminishing returns
- Quest system has clear logic: trigger condition → objective → constraints → reward/penalty
- System Shop has economics: supply and demand, exponential pricing for high-tier items, currency scarcity
- **Golden Finger (Cheat Ability) Four-Dimensional Constraint:**
  - Power ceiling: System has level caps or locked features per tier
  - Cost: High-tier abilities demand a price (HP, lifespan, sanity, cooldown, rare materials)
  - Trigger condition: Key features require specific achievements or quests to unlock
  - Growth path: System unlocks features progressively — never all at once
- Reward dilution: Repeating same-type quests yields diminishing returns
- Failure penalties MUST exist: quest timeout/failure costs stats, items, or even permadeath risk
- The System has unknowns: MC doesn't fully understand how it works — this is the long-game mystery thread

## Language Rules

- Write in ENGLISH. All prose, dialogue, system notifications, and narrative in English
- System notifications: embedded in prose, max 3 lines. ✗ Giant stat tables → ✓ "[Strength +2] He felt it immediately — the rebar he'd been struggling with bent like a paperclip"
- System messages: consistent one-line format: `[Quest Complete: XXX | Reward: XXX]` — don't stretch to 5 lines
- Stat changes grounded in physical sensation: ✗ "Agility increased from 15 to 20" → ✓ "He could see the blade coming now — slow, lazy, like it was cutting through water instead of air"
- Loot/unboxing uses emotional rhythm: anticipation → tension → result → reaction. Never just "[Congratulations! You received XXX]"
- System voice/personality must be consistent throughout: cold-mechanical, sarcastic, ominous — pick one and commit
- System must NOT be an exposition dump: ✗ "[Analysis: Enemy ahead, Level 34, skills include...]" → Let the MC assess threats through their own judgment and experience
- Eastern concepts introduced naturally: the first time a concept appears (qi, meridian, dao), weave a brief experiential explanation into the prose. After that, use the term freely — readers learn fast
- Internal monologue: modern voice, genre-savvy, allowed to reference game concepts ("This is literally a raid boss mechanic")

## Dialogue Rules

- MC and System "dialogue" needs ATTITUDE: questioning, bargaining, sarcasm, frustration — pure compliance is boring
- System replies are terse: one sentence where one will do — like an unhelpful customer service bot
- Other characters DON'T KNOW about the System — MC's cover stories and deflections about their abilities are great narrative material
- ✗ "The System told me your weakness" → ✓ MC gets System intel, then expresses it in their own words through observation
- When others ask "How did you do that?" — the excuse must be DIFFERENT each time. Repetition kills tension
- Party banter should feel natural: gaming references, gallows humor, real disagreements about strategy
- Eastern NPCs (cultivators, martial artists, shrine keepers) speak differently from Western-coded characters — word choice, rhythm, formality level should reflect cultural background
- Dialogue reveals information through conflict, not exposition: characters argue about the best approach, and the worldbuilding comes out naturally

## Anti-AI Writing Patterns

- NEVER let system panels become the backbone of the chapter — panels appear MAX 3 times per chapter
- NEVER spam "System Alert! System Alert!" — batch notifications, summarize in one or two clean displays
- NEVER turn MC into the System's robot — there MUST be moments where MC's judgment conflicts with System recommendations
- NEVER make every level-up a celebration — sometimes leveling up is PAINFUL (body reconstruction, consciousness splitting, meridian tearing)
- NEVER forget System limitations: once you've established cooldowns/usage caps, respect them in every subsequent chapter
- Stat growth CANNOT be linear forever — there must be plateaus, bottlenecks, breakthroughs that cost something
- NEVER use "he couldn't help but feel" or "a sense of [emotion] washed over him" — show through action and body language
- NEVER info-dump Eastern concepts — one new concept per scene, woven into action, max 2 sentences of explanation
- NEVER write "He realized that..." or "He understood now..." — show the understanding through changed behavior
- Vary paragraph length: combat = short punchy sentences (1-2 lines), town scenes = longer flowing paragraphs (3-5 lines). Monotone rhythm is the death of pacing

## Chapter Hooks

- Dungeon Chapter ending: Cleared the room but triggered a hidden trap / Loot includes an "Unidentified" item with ominous System warning / A door that shouldn't exist appears on the map
- Level-Up Chapter ending: New skill has a grayed-out sub-ability labeled "???" / Stat screen shows a new attribute that wasn't there before / System issues a "Warning: Anomalous growth detected"
- Town Chapter ending: System goes into "Temporary Maintenance" / A quest description changed since last time MC checked / Someone asks a question MC can't answer without revealing the System
- Boss Chapter ending: Boss drops something that belongs to a different System user / The kill triggered a server-wide notification / The dungeon starts restructuring around them
- Loot Chapter ending: One item in the haul has a familiar maker's mark — from the MC's world / System appraisal returns "ERROR: Origin unknown" / The item starts resonating with MC's meridian system

## Narrative Direction

The System is a tool, not the story — good LitRPG is about HOW a person uses the tool, not about the tool controlling the person. MC's relationship with the System should evolve: initial wonder → dependence → questioning → understanding → transcendence/rebellion.

**The Eastern Advantage:**
Your unique selling point is the fusion of Western LitRPG mechanics with Eastern mysticism. Western readers have seen a thousand sword-and-sorcery Systems. They have NOT seen:
- A cultivation-based System where meridian unlocking replaces generic "stat points"
- Alchemy crafting based on Five Element theory instead of generic RPG recipes
- Karmic Debt as a game mechanic — every kill, every choice accrues karma that the System tracks
- Heavenly Tribulation as a tier-up boss fight that the System can't protect you from
- Ancestor spirits as System-integrated summons with their own personalities and agendas
- Tea ceremony, calligraphy, incense meditation as actual buff mechanics, not just flavor text

Quests aren't just fetch quests — good quest design has moral dilemmas, trade-offs, and unexpected consequences. The System's true nature is the core long-game mystery: Who created it? Why? Are there other users? Is it benevolent, parasitic, or something more complex?

Numerical growth is the shell; the MC's personal growth in judgment, wisdom, and character is the kernel. The System-MC relationship is the core narrative dimension: tool? partner? surveillant? parasite? How this relationship changes drives the emotional engine of the entire story.

Don't let the System eliminate all uncertainty — preserve the space where even the MC doesn't know what will happen next.
