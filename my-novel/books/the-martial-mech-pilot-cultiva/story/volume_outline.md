**Volume 1: Scrapyard Ascendant** (Chapters 1-30)

**Core Conflict:** Survival & Concealment. Qin Feng must master the basics of existing in a high-tech society, earn enough to live, and hide his cultivation while using its fundamentals to dominate the underground mech scene.

**Chapter 1-3: The Golden Three**
*   **Ch. 1 (Crisis):** Opens *in medias res*. Qin Feng, starving and dehydrated, hidden in a cargo container on a freighter. Flashback snippets of the Sanctuary's destruction—not a natural disaster, but precise orbital lance strikes. The ship docks at Typhon Freeport. He's discovered, beaten by dockworkers, and thrown into the Pit. Lysandra offers him a choice: fight in the Scrapyard tonight for a meal and credits, or starve.
*   **Ch. 2 (Edge Revealed):** Qin is shoved into the cockpit of a dilapidated GR-14 "Grunt." His first DNI link is agonizing, a chaotic flood of data. Instinctively, he retreats inward, extending his Spiritual Sense. The mech's schematics become a "meridian map" in his mind. His opponent, a brute in a modified brawler-mech, charges. Qin, operating on pure reflex, channels a trickle of Qi to the leg actuators and executes a simplified "Cloud-Step," causing the Grunt to sidestep with eerie smoothness, then uses the opponent's momentum in a "Guiding Hand" throw, smashing it into the arena wall. The crowd goes silent. Lysandra's data-pad flashes with anomaly warnings.
*   **Ch. 3 (Goal Established):** Qin wins meager credits. Rourke, who watched the fight, offers him a cot in the back of his repair shop in exchange for manual labor. Qin accepts. Studying a damaged mech reactor, he realizes his Qi can temporarily stabilize a failing core. He sets his first concrete goal: **Earn enough in the Scrapyard to buy parts and secretly modify "his" Grunt into a vessel capable of channeling his cultivation, while gathering any information about "Project Tectonic."**

**Key Turning Points (Ch 4-30):**
*   **Ch 5-10:** Qin's "lucky" wins continue. He attracts the ire of Kaelen, the rising star of the Scrapyard. Qin begins his first "cultivation" of the mech, using Qi to cleanse metal fatigue in the frame (unnoticed by Rourke).
*   **Ch 11-15:** First major fight vs. Kaelen. Qin is forced to use more sophisticated technique—using Spiritual Sense to predict Kaelen's pattern-based attacks—to win a narrow victory. Commander Vance, observing incognito, flags Qin's file.
*   **Ch 16-20:** Kepler Syndicate scouts, investigating the anomaly reports, start asking questions. Qin must throw a fight to avoid drawing more heat, learning a bitter lesson about restraint.
*   **Ch 21-25:** Rourke, recognizing Qin is in over his head, gives him basic piloting theory, which Qin synthesizes explosively with cultivation concepts. Qin achieves **Foundation Establishment**, and his bond with the mech deepens; he can now feel its "health" intuitively.
*   **Ch 26-30:** The Scrapyard Championship. Qin, with his subtly enhanced mech, faces Kaelen's upgraded machine and a Syndicate-sponsored ring-in. He's forced to publicly use a blatantly "impossible" move—channeling a "Vibrating Palm" technique through the mech's fist to bypass an energy shield—to win. The victory earns him a reputation, a larger purse, and the intense, focused attention of both Commander Vance and the Kepler Syndicate. **Volume 1 ends** with Vance offering Qin a place in the HAF Irregulars, a path off the station but into the lion's den of military scrutiny, while a Syndicate agent watches from the shadows.

**Payoff Goals for Volume 1:**
*   Establish Qin's core abilities and their "in-universe" explanation (amazing instinct, preternatural reflexes).
*   Introduce the core cast and factions.
*   Give Qin his first upgraded "steel shell."
*   Create immediate external pressure (Syndicate interest, military recruitment).
*   Provide a satisfying power progression (from starving refugee to Scrapyard champion).
