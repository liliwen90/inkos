## 01_World-Building
**Era:** The 3rd Millennium, Galactic Standard Calendar. Humanity has spread across the Orion Arm, governed by the powerful Terran Hegemony and rival corporate conglomerates like the Kepler Syndicate. The primary instrument of war, exploration, and industry is the Mech: 8 to 20-meter tall humanoid war machines piloted via Direct Neural Interface (DNI).

**Core Technology:** DNI allows pilots to "become" their mech, but it's a crude translation of nerve impulses to servos and hydraulics. Pilots are trained to think in terms of system commands (e.g., "activate left shoulder thruster at 40%"). Energy is provided by compact fusion cores. Defenses are layered energy shields and composite armor. The human body is seen as a fragile, inefficient vessel; peak physical conditioning is for DNI compatibility, not combat prowess.

**The Forgotten Truth:** In the Great Exodus from a dying Earth, the knowledge of Cultivation—the art of refining one's internal life force (Qi) and spirit (Shen)—was lost, relegated to myth. "Martial arts" are viewed as primitive calisthenics. The truth is that the laws of Qi and spiritual energy are a fundamental, overlooked layer of reality, a "bio-resonant field" that advanced sensors misinterpret as background noise.

**The Power System - Dual Paths:**
1.  **Standard Pilot Path:** Relies on DNI proficiency, tactical programming, and mech specs. Ranks: Cadet → Junior Pilot → Pilot → Ace → Knight (awarded for valor) → Lord-Commander.
2.  **Cultivation Path (MC's Cheat):** Qin Feng's path, treating the mech as an external Dantian and spiritual artifact.
    *   **Stages:** Qi Condensation (gathering energy) → Foundation Establishment (solidifying core, can begin mech bonding) → Core Formation (forming a "Pilot Core," qualitative leap in control) → Nascent Soul (soul can partially inhabit mech, remote operation possible) → and beyond (lost knowledge).
    *   **Integration:** Qi can be channeled into mech systems, boosting output beyond specs. Spiritual Sense provides 360-degree perception and intuitive threat prediction. Martial techniques become mech combat forms (e.g., "Mountain-Sundering Fist" channeled through a mech's fist).

**Core Rule:** Cultivation is not magic. It is a sophisticated science of energy manipulation that the galactic civilization has forgotten. Its principles can logically enhance technology: Qi flow optimizes energy conduits, formation arrays can be programmed into shield harmonics, and spiritual forging can repair and enhance metal on a molecular level.

## 02_Protagonist
**Name:** Qin Feng (Westernized: Feng Qin)
**Identity:** The last disciple of the "Kunlun Sanctuary," a hidden pocket dimension that preserved a fragment of Earth's ancient cultivation civilization. He is 17, a refugee with no legal identity in the Hegemony.

**Special Ability / Cheat:** **[The Cultivator's Paradigm]**
*   **Spiritual Sense Over DNI:** His consciousness doesn't just *link* to a mech; it *envelops* it. He perceives the mech as an extension of his own body, feeling stress points, energy flow, and external stimuli with perfect clarity, far beyond DNI feedback limits.
*   **Qi as Hyper-Efficient Fuel:** His internal Qi can be injected into a mech's power system, temporarily overriding safety limiters and providing bursts of performance that defy engineering specs. It also allows him to operate a mech with a dead or damaged core for a limited time.
*   **Martial Principles as Combat Algorithms:** His martial arts are not rote movements but expressions of physics-defying principles (leverage, momentum transfer, kinetic energy redirection). Applied through a mech, they result in "impossible" maneuvers.

**Personality Core:**
*   **Resilient & Adaptable:** Forced from a monastic, tradition-bound life into a brutal, high-tech underworld. He learns quickly, observing and mimicking to survive.
*   **Quietly Proud & Stoic:** Carries the weight of being the last of his kind. His pride is internal, manifesting as a refusal to be looked down upon and an unwavering confidence in his path's superiority.
*   **Analytical & Introspective:** Approaches new technology and combat styles like a scholar, deconstructing them to see how cultivation principles can apply. His internal monologue often translates sci-fi concepts into cultivation terms.
*   **Driven by Duty & Vengeance:** His core drive is twofold: to survive and grow strong enough to uncover who destroyed the Sanctuary (a Kepler Syndicate mining operation, "Project Tectonic") and to find a way to rebuild his legacy.

**Behavioral Boundaries:**
*   **Will NOT** recklessly reveal his nature. He understands he would be dissected or weaponized.
*   **Will NOT** abandon his cultivation, even when it seems "useless" or "backwards."
*   **Will NOT** kill indiscriminately. His upbringing instilled discipline. He will disable, but lethal force is reserved for clear, dire threats.
*   **Will** prioritize survival and incremental growth over flashy, risky displays of power.

## 03_Factions-and-Characters
**Major Factions:**
*   **Terran Hegemony:** The dominant human polity. Its military, the Hegemony Armed Forces (HAF), is the pinnacle of conventional mech warfare. They value order, protocol, and technological supremacy.
*   **Kepler Syndicate:** A mega-corporation with quasi-governmental power. Ruthless, profit-driven, and the indirect destroyer of the Sanctuary (via a subsidiary). They pioneer experimental tech and employ mercenaries.
*   **Freeport Alliance:** A loose coalition of independent space stations, asteroid colonies, and mercenary bands. Lawless, opportunistic, and where our story begins. Home to the "Scrapyard" fight circuit.

**Key Supporting Characters:**
*   **Rourke (The Veteran):** A grizzled, retired HAF mechanic who runs a repair shop in the Freeport's lower decks. **Role:** Qin Feng's first contact and unwitting benefactor. **Motivation:** To drink away his regrets and be left alone. **Relationship:** Initially hires Qin for grunt work, sees his uncanny "knack" with machines. Becomes a reluctant mentor/father figure. **Goal:** To protect his small, found family (Qin, others) from the port's dangers.
*   **Lysandra (The Fixer):** A sharp-eyed information broker and talent scout for the Scrapyard fights. **Role:** Qin Feng's entry into the underground mech scene. **Motivation:** To find and profit from "diamonds in the rough" before the big leagues do. **Relationship:** Sees Qin as a fascinating, high-risk investment. Pragmatic, not sentimental. **Goal:** To build her own network and escape the Freeport's underbelly.
*   **Kaelen (The Rival):** A talented, arrogant young pilot from a minor noble house, slumming it in the Scrapyard to prove himself. **Role:** Early antagonist and measuring stick. **Motivation:** To earn glory and restore his family's status through sheer piloting skill. **Relationship:** Despises Qin's "backwater" demeanor and is infuriated by his inexplicable successes. **Goal:** To publicly crush Qin and use the victory as a stepping stone.
*   **Commander Alistair Vance (The Officer):** A by-the-book HAF officer recruiting for a new "Irregulars" battalion, scouting the Freeport for raw talent. **Role:** Qin's potential ticket into the legitimate (but dangerous) world. **Motivation:** To build an effective unit by any means necessary, frustrated by HAF bureaucracy. **Relationship:** Intrigued by Qin's anomalous fight data. Sees potential but is deeply suspicious. **Goal:** To turn undisciplined talent into soldiers.

## 04_Geography-and-Environment
**Primary Starting Location: The Freeport of Typhon.**
A massive, ramshackle space station built from salvaged starship hulls and asteroid ore. It is a layered society:
*   **The Spire:** Upper decks, clean, with corporate offices and legitimate trade.
*   **The Mid-Deck Bazaar:** Crowded markets, basic housing, and entertainment.
*   **The Underbelly (The Pit):** Lower decks, where life support is spotty, law is enforced by gang bosses, and the **Scrapyard** arena is located.

**The Scrapyard:** A repurposed cargo bay. The "ring" is a debris-strewn field of wrecked mechs and industrial junk. Mechs used are battered training models (like the **GR-14 "Grunt"**) or cobbled-together wrecks, held together by hope and spare parts. The atmosphere is thick with ozone, sweat, and the roar of a bloodthirsty crowd.

**Key Environmental Feature: "Signal Dampening Field."** A side-effect of the station's patchwork construction and mineral composition. It disrupts long-range sensors and comms, creating pockets of "static" that Qin Feng's Spiritual Sense can penetrate more easily than standard DNI, giving him a unique local advantage.

## 05_Title-and-Synopsis
**Title:** **Iron Pulse: The Last Cultivator** (Alternative: **Cultivator in a Steel Shell**)
*   **Rationale:** "Iron Pulse" signals the fusion of metal (mech) and life force/energy (pulse). "The Last Cultivator" immediately establishes the unique selling point and the protagonist's lonely, high-stakes position. It's hook-driven and clear on genre.

**Synopsis:**
Qin Feng is the last survivor of a forgotten cultivation sect, hiding in the grimy underbelly of a lawless space station after his home was obliterated from orbit. To eat, he must fight in the deadly Scrapyard, piloting a junk-heap mech against killers with neural implants and laser swords. But where they see a machine, Qin sees a potential divine artifact; where they use computer-assisted targeting, he uses spiritual sense and ancient martial forms. When his battered training mech moves with impossible grace, shattering a superior opponent with a single, precise blow, the station's power brokers take notice. Now, Qin must navigate a world of corporate spies and military recruiters, hiding his true power while using it to climb from the scrap heap to the stars—and find the ones who destroyed everything he knew.
