# Chapter 2 Plan

## Chapter Title
The Neural Maze

## POV
Qin Feng / Third-person limited

## Scene Beats
1. **Mech Bay Introduction** - Lysandra leads Qin through the Scrapyard's preparation area, a chaotic maze of salvaged mechs and jury-rigged equipment. She explains the basics: six hours to learn the GR-14 "Grunt" systems, neural interface hookup, and survival rules. Qin observes other fighters prepping - some are seasoned veterans with scars and cybernetic implants, others are desperate newcomers like himself. (~400 words)

2. **First DNI Attempt** - Alone in the cockpit of his assigned Grunt, Qin attempts his first Direct Neural Interface connection. The experience is overwhelming - a chaotic flood of system diagnostics, targeting data, and mechanical feedback that feels like having his consciousness shredded. He instinctively retreats inward, using meditation techniques to center himself. (~350 words)

3. **Cultivation Paradigm Shift** - Drawing on his cultivation knowledge, Qin reframes the mech as a potential external dantian. He extends his Spiritual Sense cautiously, mapping the mech's "meridians" (power conduits) and "pressure points" (critical system junctions). The machine's schematic becomes intuitive rather than overwhelming. He discovers the Grunt's fusion core is failing - a detail missed by standard diagnostics. (~500 words)

4. **Qi Stabilization Experiment** - Carefully channeling a thread of his precious Qi reserves, Qin attempts to stabilize the reactor's fluctuations. The process is exhausting and risky - one mistake could cause an explosion. He succeeds in temporarily smoothing the power flow, but the effort leaves him drained and with a splitting headache. The mech's performance readouts show marginal improvements that he hopes won't be noticed. (~450 words)

5. **Combat Training Session** - Lysandra returns to run Qin through basic mech combat drills. Using his new understanding, Qin's movements are surprisingly fluid for a beginner, though he carefully masks his true capability. He practices basic maneuvers while secretly testing how cultivation principles translate to hydraulics and servo motors. Lysandra notices his "natural talent" but attributes it to desperation and adrenaline. (~400 words)

## Emotional Curve
Overwhelming confusion and technological shock → Breakthrough moment of understanding through cultivation lens → Cautious hope mixed with exhaustion and awareness of the dangers ahead

## Subplots Advanced
- 废料场生存: Qin makes his first concrete progress toward surviving the arena fight by successfully interfacing with his mech and improving its performance, though at significant personal cost

## Hooks Resolved
- Ch.1 H006 (Scrapyard mech fights): Partially resolved - Qin has learned to pilot and enhanced his mech, setting up for the actual fight

## New Hooks Set
- Qin's Qi reserves are now critically low after stabilizing the reactor - he needs to find a way to recover before the fight
- The enhanced mech performance may attract unwanted attention from arena technicians
- Qin realizes that fully integrating cultivation with mech piloting could revolutionize both, but the knowledge is dangerous

## Target Word Count
2200 words

## Planning Rationale
This chapter fulfills the immediate promise from Chapter 1 - showing how Qin will learn to pilot a mech in six hours. It advances Hook H004 (cultivation-mech integration) by showing his first successful synthesis of the two systems, while setting up the resource management challenge that will drive tension in the upcoming fight. The chapter maintains the progression fantasy principle that power comes at a cost (Qi depletion) and shows concrete skill development rather than just exposition. The emotional arc moves from technological overwhelm to cautious mastery, building reader confidence in Qin's abilities while establishing the stakes for his depleted condition going into combat.
