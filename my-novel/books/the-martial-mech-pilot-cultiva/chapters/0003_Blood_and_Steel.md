# Chapter 3: Blood and Steel

The Grunt's cockpit reeked of ozone, stale sweat, and hydraulic fluid that had seeped into every surface. Qin Feng sat in the pilot's seat, eyes closed, hands resting on his knees. The neural crown lay beside him—cold metal and promise.

He breathed.

In through the nose, drawing air deep into his dantian. Out through the mouth, releasing tension. The Four Pillars technique Master Chen had drilled into him during those first meditation sessions. Each cycle pulled calm from chaos.

His Qi reserves hung dangerously low. Repairing the fusion core had drained him to an ache that settled in his bones. Spiritual energy that should flow through his meridians in steady currents now trickled, weak and thin. Three minutes of enhanced control. Maybe less under pressure.

*One hundred and eighty seconds to survive two experienced pilots.*

Through the viewing port, he watched them prepare.

Kozlov's mech stood thirty meters left—heavier than the Grunt, reinforced shoulder armor gleaming under the harsh lights. Twin-barreled autocannons mounted on each arm. The veteran moved with methodical precision, running his pre-combat checklist. Weapon systems cycled. Actuators tested their range. Every movement spoke of years, of battles fought and survived. Kozlov would be predictable but solid. A mountain.

Reaper's machine crouched to his right like a predator ready to spring. Brawler-class frame, heavily modified. Reinforced knuckles on its manipulator claws. Extra thrusters at hips and shoulders for sudden acceleration bursts. The mech twitched, as if the pilot couldn't contain their hunger. Where Kozlov was methodical, Reaper was storm.

Qin Feng extended his Spiritual Sense, careful not to waste energy.

The arena beyond the preparation bay gates sprawled—a debris field of wrecked mech parts, collapsed scaffolding, chunks of structural plating from abandoned repairs. Multiple levels created by stacked cargo containers, some intact, others torn open to reveal tangled interiors. Good cover. Terrain that favored maneuverability over raw firepower.

*The Grunt is lighter. More agile.*

He pulled his awareness back. The crowd's noise filtered through the bay doors—a low rumble building toward midnight. Hundreds of voices, maybe thousands. Betting odds would be flashing somewhere, numbers shifting with last-minute wagers. He wondered what the line was on the unknown refugee in the battered training mech.

Probably not favorable.

At exactly midnight, the bay doors hissed open. Artificial light flooded in, harsh and glaring. Lysandra stepped into the light, her amplified voice carrying over the crowd's murmur.

"Ladies and gentlemen! Scum and nobility! Welcome to the midnight special!"

The answering roar shook the deck plating. Qin Feng felt the vibration through the Grunt's frame.

"Three pilots! One arena! Only one walks away with the purse!" Lysandra's voice took on theatrical edge. "To my left, a veteran of two years—the man who believes firepower solves every problem! Give it up for Kozlov!"

Spotlights swung to Kozlov's mech. The veteran raised one autocannon. The crowd cheered.

"To my right, our newest contender! Arrived last week and already made a reputation for aggressive, close-quarters brutality! They call him Reaper!"

Reaper's mech slammed its fists together in a shower of sparks. The crowd's response was louder, more bloodthirsty.

"And in the center..." Lysandra paused. "A mystery. No record. No history. Climbed out of a cargo container three days ago and decided he wanted to play with the big boys. They're calling him... the Stowaway."

Mixed reaction—laughter, boos, a few curious cheers. Qin Feng kept the Grunt still. Let them underestimate. Let them think fresh meat.

"Rules are simple! Disable your opponents. Yield when your mech can't continue. Try not to die—medical bills cut into our profits. Last mech standing takes the purse: five thousand credits, a month's berth in the habitation blocks, and bragging rights until next week."

Five thousand credits. More money than he'd ever seen. Enough to eat properly. To buy information. To plan beyond mere survival.

"Pilots! Prepare for neural link!"

Qin Feng lifted the neural crown. Contact points pressed cold against his scalp. He took one last breath, centering himself.

*I've survived this long.*

The connection engaged.

No disorientation this time. No flood of chaotic data. His Spiritual Sense flowed naturally into the machine, mapping systems, feeling the fusion core's steady pulse, hydraulic fluid moving through lines like blood through veins. The Grunt wasn't separate anymore—it was extension of will. Heavy, yes. Limited, certainly. But his.

The bay doors fully retracted. Three mechs stood at the arena's edge, spotlights painting them in stark relief against the debris field beyond.

"Begin!"

Kozlov moved first.

His heavier mech wasn't fast, but it didn't need to be. Both autocannons tracked onto Qin Feng's position and opened fire. High-explosive rounds tore through the air, leaving tracer trails.

Qin Feng didn't think.

A thread of Qi flowed into the Grunt's leg actuators. The machine sidestepped with smoothness that defied its battered appearance, rounds impacting deck plating where he'd stood a heartbeat before. He kept moving—constant motion, weaving between debris chunks, using the collapsed crane arm for cover.

*Predictable. Leading his shots, assuming straight-line movement.*

Qin Feng changed direction mid-stride, reversing momentum in a move that should have torn the mech's knee joints. The Grunt's frame groaned but held. Rounds slammed into the crane arm behind him, tearing through rusted metal.

He could feel his Qi draining. The enhanced maneuver had cost him. Two and a half minutes left.

Reaper entered the fight.

The brawler-class mech shot forward, thrusters firing in controlled bursts. It didn't go for Qin Feng directly—instead, it cut across the arena, positioning itself between Qin and his cover.

Pincer movement. Kozlov providing suppressing fire, Reaper closing for melee. They'd decided to eliminate the unknown factor first.

Smart. Ruthless. Exactly what he would have done.

Qin Feng guided the Grunt behind cargo containers just as autocannon fire chewed into their far side. The impacts sounded like giant hammers striking anvil. Metal screamed as rounds punched through.

He could hear Reaper approaching—the distinctive whine of overclocked actuators, heavy footfalls shaking the deck.

*They're working together temporarily, but they don't trust each other.*

Qin Feng waited until the last moment, then pushed the Grunt out from cover—not away from Reaper, but toward him.

The move caught the aggressive pilot off guard. Reaper's mech hesitated for a fraction of a second, programming conflicting between attack and defense.

In that hesitation, Qin Feng channeled another thread of Qi. The Grunt's manipulator claw shot out, grabbing debris—a torn section of plating the size of a small vehicle. He swung it like a shield just as Kozlov's next volley arrived.

Rounds impacted the plating, tearing through but slowing enough that they only scored the Grunt's shoulder armor instead of penetrating the cockpit. The force slammed through the mech's frame, rattling Qin in his harness.

He didn't fight the momentum. Instead, he guided it, using the impact to spin the Grunt around, releasing the shredded plating directly into Reaper's path.

The brawler mech had to swerve, losing its aggressive charge. It crashed through a scrap pile instead, sending metal fragments flying.

Two minutes of Qi remaining.

Kozlov adjusted tactics. The autocannons fell silent. The veteran's mech began advancing, using its heavier armor to push through debris rather than navigate around it. Steady, relentless approach. He'd realized that trying to hit the agile Grunt at range wasted ammunition.

Reaper extracted his mech from the scrap pile, movements jerky with anger. The brawler charged again, more cautiously this time.

Qin Feng backed the Grunt through the debris field, keeping both opponents in view. His Spiritual Sense gave him perfect awareness of the terrain behind him—every piece of cover, every weak deck plate, every elevation change.

But awareness wasn't enough. He needed an advantage.

The Grunt's autocannon was crude with limited ammunition and that persistent rightward drift. Against Kozlov's heavier armor, it would be ineffective unless he scored a perfect hit on a joint or sensor cluster. Against Reaper's speed, he'd be lucky to hit at all.

*Don't use it as a weapon. Use it as distraction.*

He guided the Grunt up a ramp of collapsed plating to a higher level—a platform created by the remains of a cargo hauler's loading deck. The position gave him better field of view but also made him more exposed.

Kozlov took the bait. The veteran's mech raised its autocannons, tracking Qin's position.

At the same moment, Reaper's mech activated its thrusters, launching itself up toward the platform in a burst of acceleration.

Qin Feng waited. Counted heartbeats.

Just as Kozlov fired, and just as Reaper reached the apex of his jump, Qin Feng channeled Qi into the Grunt's legs and pushed.

The Grunt dropped off the platform's edge, falling rather than jumping. Autocannon rounds passed through the space where he'd been a moment before—and intersected with Reaper's ascending mech.

The brawler-class machine had no energy shields. The rounds meant for Qin Feng struck it instead, tearing into its torso armor. Reaper's mech twisted in mid-air, thrusters firing wildly as the pilot tried to regain control. It crashed onto the platform, skidding across metal surface in a shower of sparks.

Kozlov immediately shifted fire, trying to finish the wounded Reaper.

*Now.*

Qin Feng guided the Grunt through the debris at ground level, moving fast. His Qi was draining rapidly—maybe forty seconds left. The strain of maintaining both Spiritual Sense and enhanced control was taking its toll. A headache began to build behind his eyes, sharp and insistent.

He emerged from cover behind Kozlov's mech. The veteran was still focused on Reaper, pouring fire into the damaged brawler.

Qin Feng didn't have the firepower to penetrate Kozlov's rear armor. But he didn't need to.

He channeled the last of his Qi into the Grunt's manipulator claw, focusing not on strength, but on precision. The claw shot forward, not striking armor, but finding the gap between armor plates at the mech's left hip—the joint where the leg actuator connected to the torso.

The Grunt's claw wasn't designed for surgical strikes. It was a crude tool for lifting and crushing. But guided by Spiritual Sense and the last dregs of cultivation energy, it found its target.

Qin Feng twisted.

Metal screamed. Hydraulic lines ruptured, spraying fluid across the deck. Kozlov's mech staggered as its left leg lost power, the actuator damaged beyond immediate function.

The veteran reacted quickly, swinging his torso around to bring his autocannons to bear. But with only one functioning leg, the movement was slow, unbalanced.

Qin Feng was already moving, guiding the Grunt back into cover behind the collapsed crane arm as Kozlov's wild return fire tore chunks from the debris around him.

His Qi was gone. The emptiness that followed was profound—a hollow ache deeper than physical exhaustion. His Spiritual Sense retreated to normal range, the mech once again feeling like a separate entity rather than extension of himself. His hands began to tremble against the controls, vision blurring slightly at the edges.

But the battle wasn't over.

Reaper's mech pushed itself up from the platform. Torso armor shredded, hydraulic fluid leaking from multiple wounds, but still functional. Still dangerous.

Kozlov's mech stood on one leg, autocannons tracking between his two opponents. The veteran had to know his mobility was crippled. He was vulnerable.

For a long moment, the three mechs stood in tense triangle, each waiting for another to make the first move.

The crowd had fallen silent, sensing the shift.

Reaper moved first. Not toward Qin Feng, but toward Kozlov.

The veteran saw it coming. Both autocannons fired, rounds tearing into the already damaged brawler. But Reaper didn't stop. The aggressive pilot pushed through the fire, closing the distance, manipulator claws reaching for Kozlov's damaged leg.

Kozlov tried to back away, but with only one functioning leg, he could only hobble. Reaper's claws closed around the damaged actuator and pulled.

The sound was like a tree being torn from the ground. Kozlov's mech toppled, crashing onto its side. The veteran's cockpit hatch popped open—the yield signal.

Reaper turned toward Qin Feng.

The brawler mech was badly damaged. Torso armor hung in shreds. One manipulator claw was bent at an unnatural angle. Hydraulic fluid painted its legs in slick, dark streaks. But it was still standing. Still armed.

And Qin Feng had no Qi left. No enhanced control. No Spiritual Sense beyond his normal human range. His hands shook against the controls, and each movement of the Grunt felt sluggish, delayed by his exhausted reactions.

Just the Grunt. And himself.

Reaper charged.

Pure aggression—no finesse, no strategy. The desperate lunge of a wounded predator going for the kill.

Qin Feng didn't try to evade. He couldn't—the Grunt's response time without Qi enhancement was too slow, and his own reactions were compromised by spiritual depletion.

Instead, he did something Reaper didn't expect.

He charged back.

The two mechs closed the distance. Reaper's functional claw swung in a brutal arc aimed at the Grunt's cockpit. A killing blow.

At the last possible moment, Qin Feng guided the Grunt into a slide, the mech's legs scraping across deck plating in a shower of sparks. The claw passed over him, missing by centimeters.

He didn't try to stand. Instead, he used the Grunt's manipulator claw to strike upward as he slid beneath Reaper's mech—not at armor, but at the already damaged hip joint on the brawler's leg.

The impact wasn't powerful. But it was precise. And it hit exactly where previous damage had weakened the structure.

Reaper's mech staggered. The damaged leg buckled.

Qin Feng guided the Grunt back to its feet, turning to face his opponent. Both mechs stood damaged, leaking fluids, systems strained to their limits.

For a heartbeat, they faced each other.

Then Reaper's cockpit hatch hissed open. The yield.

Silence filled the arena. Complete, utter silence.

Then the crowd erupted.

The noise was physical—a wall of sound that shook the Grunt's frame. Cheers, screams, the thunder of thousands of feet stamping on metal grating.

Qin Feng guided the Grunt to a neutral stance, the machine's systems protesting every movement. Warning lights flashed across his control panel—multiple hydraulic leaks, power fluctuations in the fusion core, structural integrity compromised in three locations.

But it was still standing. He was still alive.

Lysandra's amplified voice cut through the noise. "Ladies and gentlemen! We have a winner! The Stowaway takes it!"

Spotlights swung to focus on the Grunt. Qin Feng raised the mech's manipulator claw in acknowledgment, the movement slow and deliberate.

Through the cockpit's viewing port, he saw Lysandra on the commentator's platform. She wasn't cheering. She was studying her scanner, frowning at the readout. Then she looked up, her eyes finding his cockpit as if she could see through the armor plating directly to him.

The expression on her face wasn't triumph. It was calculation.

The crowd began to chant—crude, rhythmic pounding of sound. "Stow-away! Stow-away! Stow-away!"

Qin Feng guided the Grunt toward the exit bay, moving carefully on damaged systems. As he passed the commentator's platform, his eyes caught movement in the VIP section—a raised observation deck with reinforced glass separating wealthy spectators from the arena's grit.

A man stood there, watching. Not cheering. Not chanting. Just observing.

He wore clothes that didn't belong in the Scrapyard—tailored fabric that shimmered subtly under the lights, cut in a style Qin Feng didn't recognize but that screamed expense. His posture was relaxed, hands clasped behind his back, but his attention was focused entirely on the arena floor.

On the aftermath of the battle.

Their eyes met through two layers of glass and thirty meters of distance. The man didn't smile. Didn't nod. Just watched with the calculating gaze of someone evaluating an investment.

Then he turned and walked away, disappearing into the shadows of the VIP section.

The bay doors closed behind the Grunt, cutting off the crowd's noise. The sudden quiet was deafening.

Qin Feng disconnected the neural crown. The return to his own body was jarring—the cockpit suddenly cramped, claustrophobic. His hands trembled as he unbuckled the harness. Every muscle ached. His head throbbed with the aftermath of Qi depletion.

But he was alive. He had won.

The cockpit hatch hissed open. Lysandra stood at the base of the maintenance ladder, her scanner still in hand.

"Well," she said, her voice flat. "That was interesting."

Qin Feng climbed down, his legs unsteady. He leaned against the Grunt's leg, the metal cool through his thin clothing. "The credits."

"Will be transferred. Along with your berth assignment." Lysandra studied him. "Your mech's performance metrics don't match its specs. At all."

"Lucky breaks."

"Luck doesn't explain a twelve percent increase in actuator efficiency during the final engagement. Luck doesn't explain power readings that spike exactly when you need them to." She stepped closer, lowering her voice. "Your performance metrics are unusual. Care to explain the discrepancies?"

He met her gaze. "Desperation."

For a long moment, she just looked at him. Then she nodded slowly. "Fair enough. For now." She pocketed her scanner. "Get some rest. You've earned it. And... congratulations. You're not dead."

She walked away, leaving him alone with the Grunt.

Qin Feng pressed his hand against the mech's leg, feeling the vibration of its cooling systems. The machine had held together. Had carried him through.

He looked down at his own hands, still trembling slightly. The emptiness inside him was profound—complete depletion of his spiritual energy. It would take days to recover, maybe longer without proper food and rest.

But he had won. He had survived.

And somewhere in the VIP section, a man in expensive clothes was asking questions.
